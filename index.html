RHEA

Rhea or Rheia (/ˈriːə/; Ancient Greek: Ῥέα [r̥é.aː] or Ῥεία [r̥ěː.aː]) is a mother goddess in ancient Greek religion and mythology, the Titan daughter of the earth goddess Gaia and the sky god Uranus, himself a son of Gaia.

DAUGHTER

Rhea, or Rheia, (/ˈriːə/; Ancient Greek: Ῥέα [r̥é.aː] or Ῥεία [r̥ěː.aː]), stands as a symbol of filial devotion in ancient Greek lore. As the cherished daughter of Gaia, the earth goddess, and Uranus, the sky god, she embodies the boundless love and resilience of familial ties. Like a Titan of affection, she is woven into the fabric of the cosmos, a testament to the enduring bond between parent and child.
